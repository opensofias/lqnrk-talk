liquid anarchy

proposal, not implemented.

* publish/subscribe model for policies
* designed for completely distributed p2p systems
* liquid/delegate democracy meets panarchy/polycentric law

notable features

* every participants runs an election
* owner can veto every decision and introduce policies
* realtime decision making
* no inherent equality
* people can own multiple nodes
* nodes can be owned by organisations, robots or dogs
* virtual nodes

examples of policies

* economic sanctions/subsidies
* distributed recommendation systems
* managing collectively owned systems
